Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation used to assess whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height. By dividing a person's weight in kilograms by their height in meters squared, BMI provides a numerical value that can help categorize their weight status as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese. While BMI is a useful screening tool for general health assessment, it does not directly measure body fat or account for other factors such as muscle mass or distribution.
